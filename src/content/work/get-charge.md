## The Context

Telekom Deutschland was building out a pan-European EV charging network — 56,000+ stations across Europe. They needed a mobile app that drivers would actually use, a landing page to bring them there, and a digital brand that felt coherent across both.

The brief came to KiloKilo, the digital studio I co-founded. I led the development.

## What We Built

**The mobile app:**
The primary interface for Telekom Deutschland's EV charging customers. Find stations, start sessions, manage payments. Cross-platform iOS and Android with React Native.

**The landing page:**
Product marketing site for the charging network — built in React, designed to convert.

**Digital branding:**
Beyond the code, we shaped the digital brand strategy for GET CHARGE — visual identity, tone of voice, the connective tissue between the app and the web presence. Not just building to spec, but helping define what the product looked and felt like.

## The Scale

56,000+ charging stations across Europe, with a network that was actively growing. The app had to handle real-world complexity — station availability, network outages, roaming between providers, and the full range of payment edge cases that come with cross-border EV infrastructure.

## Outcome

A live product used by Telekom Deutschland's customers across Europe. One of the larger consumer-facing projects KiloKilo shipped — and an early signal that EV infrastructure was going to be a significant market.
